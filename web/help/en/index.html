SilverTimer

Instructions & User Guide

What is SilverTimer?

SilverTimer is a colloidal silver electrolysis calculator and timer for iOS, Android, and the web. It uses Faraday's Law of Electrolysis to compute the precise running time needed to reach a target silver concentration (in PPM) for a given volume of distilled water and a known electrode current.

The app has four tabs: Calculator, Timer, History, and Info.

What You Need

  • Distilled water — never use tap water or mineral water; dissolved minerals interfere with the process and produce impure silver
  • Two pure silver electrodes — 99.9% fine silver; wires, strips, or rods all work
  • A stable DC power source — typically delivering 0.5–3 mA for home use
  • A milliammeter — to measure the actual current flowing through the circuit
  • A clean glass container — sized to your batch volume

Step 1 — Configure Default Settings

Before your first batch, open the Settings tab (gear icon, top-right of any screen) and set:

  • Default Current (mA) — measure the actual current with your milliammeter and enter that value. This is the most important setting for accuracy. Re-measure before every batch if your power source varies.
  • Default PPM — your typical target concentration (e.g. 10 PPM). This pre-fills the Calculator so you don't have to re-enter it each time.
  • Default Volume Unit — choose mL or L depending on your usual container size.
  • Enable Notifications — turn this on so the app can alert you when the timer finishes.

Step 2 — Enter Your Parameters

Open the Calculator tab and fill in the two fields:

  1. Water Volume — the amount of distilled water in your container. Tap the mL / L toggle next to the field to switch units. You can also use the quick-select chips (e.g. 250 mL, 500 mL, 1 L).
  2. Target Concentration — the PPM level you want to reach. Common values are 10, 15, 20, or 25 PPM. Tap a preset chip for quick entry.

The current used in the calculation comes from Settings → Default Current. If today's current differs from your default, update Settings before calculating.

Step 3 — Calculate

Tap Calculate Time. The result card shows the required electrolysis duration broken down into hours, minutes, and seconds.

The formula used is Faraday's Law of Electrolysis:

time (s) = (PPM × Volume_L × F) / (M_Ag × I_A × 1000) where: F = 96,485 C/mol — Faraday constant M_Ag = 107.87 g/mol — molar mass of silver I_A = current in amperes (mA ÷ 1000) PPM = target concentration in mg/L

Tap the ƒ (formula) icon on the result card to see this breakdown inside the app at any time.

Step 4 — Start the Timer

Tap Start Timer on the result card. The app switches to the Timer tab and begins counting down using a circular progress ring.

  1. Place your silver electrodes in the water.
  2. Connect the power source and verify the current with your milliammeter.
  3. Leave the app — it continues timing correctly even in the background.

The timer anchors to the device wall clock, not a software tick counter. It stays accurate even if the app is suspended, the screen locked, or the device restarted.

Timer controls:

  • Pause — suspends the countdown (also cancels pending notifications; they are rescheduled on Resume).
  • Resume — continues from where it was paused.
  • Reset — cancels the run and returns to idle. The session is not saved to History if reset before completion.

Completion Alarm

When the timer reaches zero, SilverTimer fires an alarm-level notification that works similarly to your device's alarm clock:

  • Foreground — the alarm sound plays directly through the app and a notification is shown.
  • Background / screen locked — an OS-scheduled notification fires regardless of Do Not Disturb or battery optimisation settings.
  • Android — uses AlarmManager.setAlarmClock(), which bypasses Doze mode entirely.
  • iOS — uses InterruptionLevel.timeSensitive, which breaks through Focus modes.

Tap Reset on the completed timer screen to silence the alarm and return to idle.

Electrode Cleaning Alarms

Silver oxide builds up on the positive electrode during electrolysis. Periodic cleaning (reversing polarity) keeps particle size uniform and improves purity. SilverTimer can remind you automatically at a regular interval.

To set up cleaning alarms:

  1. Open Settings → Electrode Cleaning Alarm.
  2. Toggle Enable Cleaning Alarm on.
  3. Set the Interval (5–30 minutes or a custom value).

During a run, each cleaning alarm fires an OS notification (even when backgrounded) and shows an in-app banner. The circular progress ring also displays small markers at each alarm position.

When a cleaning alarm fires:

  1. Briefly lift both electrodes out of the water.
  2. Swap which wire connects to the positive and negative terminals (reverse polarity).
  3. Return the electrodes to the water and continue.

Session History

Every run that completes normally is automatically saved to the History tab. Each entry shows:

  • Date and time of completion
  • Water volume and target PPM
  • Current used (mA)
  • Total duration

To delete a single entry, swipe it left and tap the delete button. To clear all history, tap the trash icon in the top-right corner.

Tips for Best Results

  • Always use distilled water. Tap water contains chloride ions that react with silver to form silver chloride — a white precipitate that is not colloidal silver.
  • Verify your current before every batch. Even small deviations from the stored Default Current affect the final PPM. Update Settings if your measurement differs by more than ±0.1 mA.
  • Use a magnetic stirrer or gently stir every few minutes to keep ion concentration uniform throughout the water.
  • Keep electrodes parallel and fully submerged at a consistent distance apart throughout the run.
  • Store colloidal silver in dark glass (amber or cobalt blue) away from direct light, heat, and strong electromagnetic fields. Do not use plastic containers.
  • Clean electrodes between batches with fine steel wool or a soft cloth to remove any silver oxide buildup.
